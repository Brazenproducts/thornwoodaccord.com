Book One

Bound by Thorns

Lyra crossed into the Thornwood to save her sister. She never expected to be claimed by the lord who rules it.

Lord Calder of the Court of Ash is everything the old stories warn about: cold, beautiful, and ruthless in the way only the fae can be. The bargain he offers Lyra should be impossible to accept. But the alternative is watching her sister die in a court that sees mortals as playthings.

What follows is a slow, agonizing unraveling of every wall Lyra has ever built — and every reason Calder has for staying away from her.

Court of Ash and Hunger

The bargain is broken. The courts are fracturing. And the last thing Lyra should be doing is trusting the fae lord who bargained her freedom away in the first place.

Enter the Thornwood

The Court of Ash

The oldest and most powerful of the fae courts. Ruled by cold logic and older bargains. Lord Calder has kept its throne for three hundred years by never letting anyone close enough to matter.

🌿

The Thornwood

The wild between the courts — a forest that thinks, that grows, that hungers. It is the source of all fae magic and the thing that all three courts are quietly, desperately, secretly trying to control.

⚖️

The Accord

An ancient treaty between the courts and the mortal world — binding, unbreakable, and now fraying at every edge. No one is sure who broke it first. Everyone is sure that when it fully breaks, the consequences will be catastrophic.

🌙

The Wild Bargains

Fae bargains are binding in ways mortal contracts are not. They root themselves in the chest like thorns and pull tighter the harder you fight them. Lyra learns this the hard way.
